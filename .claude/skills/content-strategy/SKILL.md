# Content Strategy

Strategy is deciding what to make, for whom, and why — before writing anything.
Content without strategy is noise.

## Start with these questions

1. **Audience** — Who exactly is this for? What do they want, fear, and search?
2. **Goal** — Awareness, trust, leads, retention, or revenue? Pick a primary.
3. **Positioning** — What's our angle that no one else credibly owns?
4. **Proof** — Why should they believe us? (data, experience, results)
5. **Distribution** — Where does the audience already pay attention?

If you can't answer these, the content plan is premature.

## Content pillars

Define 3–5 themes you'll own. Each pillar:
- Maps to an audience need and a business goal.
- Is broad enough to sustain dozens of pieces, narrow enough to be distinctive.
- Repeats consistently so the audience knows what you stand for.

Everything you publish should ladder up to a pillar. If it doesn't fit, it's
probably off-strategy.

## The content funnel

Match format and intent to the journey stage:

- **Top (awareness)** — educational, entertaining, shareable. Optimize for reach.
- **Middle (consideration)** — comparisons, deep guides, case studies. Build trust.
- **Bottom (decision)** — demos, testimonials, pricing/ROI content. Drive action.

Don't only make bottom-funnel content; you'll have no audience to convert.

## Editorial calendar

- Set a sustainable cadence you can actually keep (consistency > volume).
- Plan by theme/pillar per week or month, not random one-offs.
- Batch creation; schedule ahead; leave room for timely/reactive posts.
- Track each piece: pillar, stage, format, channel, status, publish date.

## Distribution (create once, distribute many)

- Default to "create once, distribute everywhere." Atomize a flagship piece into
  posts, clips, threads, and email.
- Prioritize owned channels (email/site) for compounding; use social for reach.
- Repurpose top performers; double down on what works rather than always-new.

## Measurement

Tie metrics to the goal, not vanity:
- Awareness → reach, impressions, new followers.
- Engagement → saves, shares, comments, watch time (intent signals).
- Conversion → email signups, leads, trials, revenue.

Review monthly: what to do more of, stop, and test next. Let data, not opinion,
allocate effort.

## Operating principles

- Consistency compounds; sporadic brilliance doesn't.
- Distinctive and useful beats polished and generic.
- One clear audience beats trying to reach everyone.
- Ship, measure, iterate — perfect plans die in drafts.
